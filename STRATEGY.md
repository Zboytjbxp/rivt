# Skilled Trades Network Strategy

## Decision

Build a hybrid: marketplace first, network always.

The first product has to solve the urgent pain: contractors need help and tradespeople need quality work. The durable company is bigger than a job board: a trusted professional graph for the skilled trades, where people maintain profiles, build reputation, find opportunities, message peers, track credentials, and return even when they are not actively chasing a job.

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> The professional network for the skilled trades.

Sharper launch positioning:

> Find trusted trade help, side work, and local opportunities without chasing Facebook threads, texts, and staffing agencies.

Emotional center:

> Where skilled tradespeople belong.

Avoid positioning the product as a generic job board, lead marketplace, temp agency, or homeowner-service marketplace.

## Product Shape

Launch around three connected surfaces:

1. Work feed
   Contractors post jobs, tradespeople find side work, both sides see clear details before wasting time.

2. Trade network
   Profiles, portfolios, specialties, availability, message threads, local trade updates, and people-to-know suggestions.

3. Trust record
   Identity verification, license disclosure, safety credentials, job records, closeout photos, payment logs, and two-way reviews.

The network surface matters because it creates daily return behavior. The marketplace matters because it creates transactions and immediate value.

## Daily Engagement Loops

- Trade pulse: local jobsite updates, code/safety reminders, trade-specific notices, and new work near the user.
- People to know: contractors and tradespeople recommended by trade, location, tools, reviews, and availability.
- Reputation progress: completed jobs, badges, reviews, portfolio additions, and response-time improvements.
- Saved opportunities: alerts for matching jobs, nearby crews, and openings by trade.
- Messaging: job-specific threads plus profile-to-profile outreach.

## Defensibility

The moat is not the first job post. The moat is the trade graph:

- Verified profiles tied to real work history.
- Local reputation and review context.
- Completion records and photo-backed proof of work.
- Trade-specific matching data.
- Contractor-to-contractor and tradesperson-to-contractor relationships.
- Safety and certification history.
- Network density by city and trade.

Competitors can copy a job board. They have a harder time copying trusted local history if users build it here first.

## Competitive Landscape

Closest construction/trade competitors:

- ToolBelt: construction labor marketplace and contractor network. Strong warning sign because it is close to the original marketplace idea.
- Skillit: AI construction hiring platform focused on craft worker recruiting and employer hiring.
- Buildforce: construction workforce platform, strongest around electricians and contractor-reviewed workers.
- Field Nation: large on-site labor marketplace, strongest in IT field service rather than construction trades.

Adjacent staffing/workforce platforms:

- WorkMarket by ADP: contractor management, onboarding, verification, and payments for companies managing independent contractors.
- Instawork: AI-powered hourly staffing and flexible work, mainly hospitality, warehouse, retail, and light industrial.
- Bluecrew/Employbridge: W-2 staffing and workforce platform.
- Bacon Work: on-demand temp labor marketplace.

Strategic gap:

Most competitors are hiring, staffing, or workforce management systems. The opening is a daily professional network for tradespeople and contractors that includes work, reputation, trust, and local trade signal.

## MVP

Build the first customer-ready version around these flows:

- Role onboarding for contractor or tradesperson.
- Verified profile with trade, location, specialties, tools, portfolio, and availability.
- Contractor job posting with clear pay, tools, difficulty, location privacy, and legal consent.
- Tradesperson work feed with filters and one-tap interest.
- Contractor applicant review and invite flow.
- Direct messaging tied to jobs and profiles.
- Completion photos, closeout confirmation, payment log, and two-way review prompt.
- Network tab with local trade pulse, people to know, and nearby openings.
- Admin beta dashboard for jobs, feedback, flagged users, and activation metrics.

Defer until after first real users:

- Full news aggregation.
- Complex AI compliance recommendations.
- Background checks.
- Automated tax forms.
- Enterprise workforce management.
- Native mobile apps.

## Jacksonville Launch

Start narrow enough to create density.

Recommended first wedge:

- One city: Jacksonville.
- Three to five trades at first: electrical, plumbing, HVAC, carpentry, and general labor.
- One use case: short-term help and side work between contractors and skilled tradespeople.

Launch motions:

- Recruit 25-50 contractors who already hire subs or need occasional help.
- Recruit 100-200 tradespeople/subs with verified profiles and availability.
- Manually seed the first supply and demand from Facebook groups, Reddit, trade schools, supply houses, and direct outreach.
- Run concierge matching behind the scenes until the marketplace feels alive.
- Keep beta free while measuring job posts, responses, messages, and completed work.

Activation targets before expanding:

- 50 completed verified profiles.
- 20 real posted jobs.
- 3+ qualified responses per job on average.
- Under 24 hours average time to first response.
- 10 completed jobs with closeout records and reviews.

## National Expansion

Expand city by city and trade by trade.

The repeatable playbook:

- Pick a city with strong construction demand and clear trade labor pain.
- Start with the trades that generated the best Jacksonville response.
- Recruit anchor contractors first.
- Fill supply around their actual upcoming work.
- Add trade-specific community and training content after liquidity exists.

Do not expand nationally as a generic empty directory. Network effects only matter when local users can actually find each other.

## Monetization

Launch beta free.

After marketplace activity exists:

- Base subscription: low monthly price for contractors and tradespeople.
- Premium contractor plan: featured openings, faster applicant alerts, priority matching, saved crews, and advanced filters.
- Premium tradesperson plan: real-time alerts, profile boost, portfolio visibility, and early access to openings.
- Business tools later: team seats, credential tracking, payment export, compliance records, and recruiting tools.

Avoid transaction fees at launch. The promise should stay simple: no temp-agency markup and no cut of the job payment.

## Initial Focus

Start with contractors first, then recruit tradespeople around actual work.

Reason:

Tradespeople will not return without real opportunities. Contractors create the demand signal. Once there are real jobs, tradespeople have a reason to complete profiles, message, and build reputation.

The operating rhythm should be:

- Get contractor commitments.
- Seed jobs.
- Recruit matching tradespeople.
- Manually help matches close.
- Convert both sides into repeat users.
